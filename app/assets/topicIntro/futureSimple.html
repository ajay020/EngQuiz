Future Simple Tense

The future simple tense is used to talk about actions or events that will happen at some point in the future.

The future simple tense can be used to:

Make predictions

The weather forecast says that it will rain tomorrow.

Make promises

I promise I will help you with your homework.

Make offers

I'll make you a sandwich if you're hungry.

Express hope

I hope that everything will be okay.

Sentence Structure

Subject + will/shall + base form of the verb (+ rest of the sentence)

Will vs Shall

Will -> common usage

"Will" is the more commonly used word to express the future in everyday conversation and writing.

  • I will call you later.
  • It will rain tomorrow.
  • They will arrive at 9 AM.

Shall -> formality

"Shall" is considered more formal than "will."
It is often used in formal documents, legal contexts, and certain types of written or spoken English.

  • I shall submit the report by Friday.(formal)
  • Shall I book a table for two? (suggestion)
  • We shall meet at the conference next week.

am/is/are + going to + base form verb

We can express the future in two ways: "will" and going to.

Both can be used to talk about events that are planned or expected to happen in the future, but there are some subtle differences between the two.

Will is generally used to express future events that are uncertain or less planned.

Examples:

  1. I will call you later. (I haven't decided when yet, but I plan to call you at some point today.)
  2. It will rain tomorrow. (I'm not sure if it will rain tomorrow, but the meteorologist says it's likely.)
  3. He will be late. (I'm pretty sure he's going to be late, but I'm not 100% sure.)

Going to is generally used to express future events that are more certain or more planned.

  1. I am going to the store later. (I have already decided that I'm going to the store later.)
  2. We're going to have a meeting tomorrow. (The meeting has already been scheduled.)
  3. She's going to be a doctor. (She has already decided that she wants to be a doctor and has taken steps to achieve that goal.)
