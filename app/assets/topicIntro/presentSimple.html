Present Simple Tense: A Quick Guide

When to Use It:

  1. Habits and Routines: We use the Present Simple to talk about things we do regularly. For example, "I brush my teeth every morning."
  2. Facts and General Truths: It's perfect for stating facts or things that are always true. For instance, "The sun rises in the east."
  3. Scheduled Events: We can also use it to talk about scheduled events like timetables or fixed plans. "The train leaves at 9 AM."

Sentence Structure:

Making sentences in the Present Simple Tense is easy. You usually just need the base form of the verb (the form you find in the dictionary) with 's' or 'es' added for third-person singular (he, she, it).

  • Affirmative (Positive) Sentences:
    • I/You/We/They + base form of the verb
    • He/She/It + base form of the verb + 's' or 'es'
  • Negative Sentences: Use 'do not' (don't) or 'does not' (doesn't) before the base form of the verb.
    • Example: "She doesn't like spinach."
  • Questions: Start the sentence with 'do' or 'does', followed by the subject, and then the base form of the verb.
    • Example: "Do you like pizza?"
    • Example: "Does she go to school by bus?"

Examples:

Affirmative:

  • "I play soccer every Saturday."
  • "She reads books in the evening."

Negative:

  • "They don't watch scary movies."
  • "He doesn't eat meat."

Questions:

  • "Do you like pizza?"
  • "Does she go to school by bus?"

Remember, the Present Simple is your go-to tense for talking about routines, habits, and facts. It's simple, and you'll use it a lot in everyday English conversations. Keep practicing, and you'll master it in no time!
