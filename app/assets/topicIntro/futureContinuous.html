Future Continuous Tense

Actions in Progress in the Future:
It is primarily used to describe actions or events that will be ongoing or in progress at a specific future time.

Example: "At 3 PM tomorrow, I will be studying for my exam."

Sentence Structure

Subject + "will" + "be" + base form of the verb with "-ing" + (rest of the sentence).

Examples

  • Affirmative Sentences:
    • She will be studying for her exam at this time next week. (Duration of study)
    • I will be working on a project tomorrow morning. (Ongoing action)
    • They will be traveling to Europe next summer. (Ongoing travel plans)

  • Negative Sentences:
    • I will not be working tomorrow.
    • She will not be studying at the library all day.
    • They will not be playing soccer at 5pm on Saturday.

  • Interrogative Sentences:
    • Will he be acting in the sequel next year?
    • Will they be playing soccer at 5pm?
    • Will she be studying all day?

  • Interrogative-Negative Sentences:
    • Won't they be working late tonight?
    • Won't she be studying for the exam tomorrow?
    • Won't you be traveling to Paris next week?

Difference between Future Simple and Future Continuous

The future simple tense is used to talk about actions that will happen at a specific time in the future,

While the future continuous tense is used to talk about actions that will be in progress at a specific time in the future.

  • Future simple:
    • I will go to the store tomorrow.
    • He will finish his homework before dinner.
    • She will visit her grandparents next week.
  • Future continuous:
    • I will be working at 9am tomorrow.
    • She will be studying at the library all day.
    • They will be playing soccer at 5pm on Saturday.
