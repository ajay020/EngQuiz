Past Perfect Continuous Tense

The past perfect continuous tense is used to talk about an action that started in the past, continued in the past, and ended at a specific time in the past.

Sentence Structure:

The past perfect continuous tense is formed with the following structure:

Subject + had been + present participle (verb + -ing) + rest of the sentence.

Examples:

  • She had been studying for hours before the exam.
  • They had been working on the project all night.
  • By the time I arrived, he had been working for an hour.
  • Had you been practicing the piano before the concert?
  • It had been working heavily, so the streets were flooded.
