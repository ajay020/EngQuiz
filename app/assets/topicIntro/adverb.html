Adverbs

Adverbs are words that modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

They can tell us how, when, where, why, or to what degree something is done.

Here are some examples of adverbs:

  • Adverbs of manner : quickly, slowly, carefully, loudly, softly
  • Adverbs of time: now, then, soon, later, yesterday, today, tomorrow
  • Adverbs of place: here, there, everywhere, nowhere, upstairs, downstairs
  • Adverbs of reason : why, because, therefore, since, so that, in order to
  • Adverbs of degree : very, quite, extremely, too, enough
  • Adverbs of frequency: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never

How Adverbs Work:

  • Modifying Verbs: Adverbs add detail to verbs, telling us how an action occurs. For example, "She sings beautifully." Here, "beautifully" describes how she sings.
  • Modifying Adjectives: They can also describe adjectives. In "an incredibly talented artist," "incredibly" enhances the meaning of "talented."
  • Modifying Other Adverbs: Adverbs can even modify other adverbs. In "He runs very quickly," "very" emphasizes the adverb "quickly."

Adverbs can be placed before the verb, after the verb, or at the beginning of the sentence. For example:

  • Quickly run to the store.
  • I ran quickly to the store.
  • Quickly, I ran to the store.

More example:

  • She sang beautifully.
  • He walked slowly down the street.
  • The car drove quickly past us.
  • She spoke softly so as not to disturb the baby.
  • He runs very quickly
  • The dog barked loudly at the mailman.
