Present Continuous

You can use the present continuous tense to talk about:

  • Actions that are happening right now:
    • I'm eating lunch.
    • She's watching TV.
    • They're playing football.
  • Actions that are happening for a period of time that includes the present moment:
    • I'm working on a new project this week.
    • She's studying English.
    • They're building a new house.
  • Future plans that have already been made:
    • I'm flying to New York next week.
    • She's getting married in June.
    • They're having a baby in December.

Sentence structure

The present continuous tense is formed using the auxiliary verb "am," "is," or "are" and the present participle of the main verb. The present participle is the verb with "ing" on the end.

Subject + am/is/are + present participle + rest of sentence

I ___ am

He, she, it, ___ is

You,they, We ___ are

For example:

  • I'm eating a sandwich.
  • She's walking to the store.
  • They're playing video games.

Here are some more examples of sentences in the present continuous tense:

  • I'm working on a presentation for tomorrow.
  • She's cooking dinner for her family.
  • They're watching a movie in the theater.
  • I'm learning Spanish.
  • She's running a marathon next month.
  • They're building a new school in our town.
  • It's raining outside.
  • The sun is shining.
  • The wind is blowing.
