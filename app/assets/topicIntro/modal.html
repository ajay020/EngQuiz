Modal Verbs

Modal verbs are auxiliary verbs that are used to express possibility, necessity, permission, or obligation. They are used with a main verb to indicate the speaker's attitude towards the action or event.

Types of Modal Verbs:

  1. Can: This modal verb is used to express the ability or capability to do something.

    Example: She can play the piano.

  2. Could: It represents past ability or polite requests.

    Example: Could you pass me the salt?

  3. May: It expresses permission, possibility, or a polite request.

    Example 1: You may leave the room. (Permission)

    Example 2: It may rain later. (Possibility)

  4. Might: Similar to 'may,' it denotes a possibility, often with a sense of uncertainty.

    Example: He might come to the party.

  5. Must: This modal verb indicates necessity or strong obligation.

    Example: You must finish your homework.

  6. Should: It's used to provide advice or make a suggestion.

    Example: You should study for the exam.

  7. Shall: Often used in questions, offers, or suggestions, and it's more common in British English.

    Example 1: Shall we go to the park?

    Example 2: I shall do my best.

  8. Will: It expresses future events, predictions, or willingness.

    Example 1: The sun will rise tomorrow.

    Example 2: I will help you with your project.

  9. Would: It is used for polite requests, conditional statements, or expressing willingness.

    Example 1: Would you like some tea?

    Example 2: If I were you, I would apologize.

Difference between 'may' and 'might':

  1. May is used to express:

    • Stronger possibility in present and future.

  2. Maight is used to express:

    • Weaker possibility in present and future.
    • To talk about past or hypothetical possibilities

Key Points:

  • Modal verbs don't have different forms for different subjects.
  • They are always followed by the base form of the main verb.
Modal Verb Meaning Example
can ability, possibility I can swim.
could ability in the past, possibility, politeness I could swim when I was five.
may possibility, permission It may rain tomorrow.
might possibility, uncertainty I might go to the cinema tonight.
must obligation, necessity You must do your homework.
should advice, recommendation You should eat more fruits and vegetables.
will future tense, determination I will help you with your project.
