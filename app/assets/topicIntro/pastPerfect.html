Past Perfect Tense

The past perfect tense is used to describe actions or events that occurred before another action or event in the past.

When to Use the Past Perfect Tense:

Past Actions Before Other Past Actions: We use the past perfect to indicate that one action or event happened before another action in the past.

For example:

She had already finished her homework when the guests arrived.

Sentence Structure:

To form the past perfect tense, we use the past tense of the auxiliary verb "had" and add the past participle of the main verb.

Subject + had + past participle + (rest of the sentence)

Examples:

  • "I had never seen that movie before last night."
  • "They had already eaten when I got to the restaurant."
  • "By the time I arrived, she had left."
