Present Perfect Tense

The present perfect tense is used to talk about actions that started in the past and continue to the present, or actions that were completed in the past but have a result that is still relevant in the present.

When to Use the Present Perfect Tense

You can use the present perfect tense in the following situations:

  • Actions or events that happened at an unspecified time in the past but have relevance or an impact on the present.
    For example:

    I have visited Paris. (The action of visiting Paris happened at an unspecified time in the past, and it's relevant to the present.)

  • Actions or events that started in the past and continue into the present. For example:

    She has lived in this city for ten years. (She started living in the city in the past, and she still lives there now.)

  • Actions or events that have just been completed. For example:

    They have just finished their homework. (The action of finishing the homework has just been completed.)

Sentence Structure

  • Use "have" with subjects like "I," "you," "we," and "they."

  • Use "has" with subjects like "he," "she," and "it."

Subject + "have" or "has" + past participle + (rest of the sentence)

Examples

Here are some examples of sentences in the present perfect tense:

  • I have traveled to many countries.
  • She has never eaten sushi.
  • They have just graduated from university.
  • We have seen that movie before.
