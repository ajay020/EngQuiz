Articles

Articles are small words that precede nouns to indicate their definiteness or indefiniteness.

  • Indefinite article: a / an
  • Definite article: the

a/an

Use "a" before words that start with a consonant sound.

  • A book
  • A car
  • A dog
  • A house
  • A table

Use "an" before words that start with a vowel sound.

  • An apple
  • An egg
  • An umbrella
  • An hour
  • An island

Exceptions

  • Use "an" before words that start with a silent "h".
  • An hour
  • An honor
  • Use "a" before words that start with a pronounced "h" if the "h" is pronounced like a consonant.
  • A house
  • A history

In general, use "a" or "an" when you are talking about something for the first time or when you are not referring to a specific thing.

I would like to buy a book. (Any book.)

I saw a bird in the sky. (Any bird.)

Do not use "a" or "an" when you are talking about a specific thing or when you are using a plural noun.

The book is on the table. (A specific book.)

I have two dogs. (Plural noun.)

the

The definite article the is used to refer to specific or particular nouns.

For example, if I say "I want to read the book on the table," I am referring to a specific book that is already known to the listener.

  • Use the when referring to specific or particular nouns that have already been mentioned or are known to the listener.
    • I saw the man who stole my wallet. (A specific man.)
    • I live in the house on the corner. (A specific house.)
    • I'm going to the movie tonight. (A specific movie.)
  • Use the when referring to nouns that are unique or one of a kind.
    • the sun
    • the moon
    • the president
  • Use the when referring to things that are considered to be part of a group or category.
    • the members of the team
    • the students in the class
    • the animals in the zoo
  • Use the when referring to things that are considered to be general or abstract.
    • the truth
    • the future
    • the internet
  • Use the when referring to things that are superlative or the best of their kind.
    • the best player in the world
    • the most beautiful woman in the room
    • the tallest building in the city

More examples:

  • I saw the man who stole my wallet. (A specific man.)
  • I live in the house on the corner. (A specific house.)
  • I'm going to the movie tonight. (A specific movie.)
  • I want to buy a new car. (Any new car.)
  • I saw the car that you were talking about. (A specific car.)
  • I'm going to the doctor tomorrow. (A specific doctor.)
  • I'm going to see a doctor tomorrow. (Any doctor.)

Exceptions

There are a few exceptions to the rules for using articles. For example, we do not use articles with:

  • Uncountable nouns (e.g., water, air, love)
  • Proper nouns (e.g., John, Mary, London)
  • Nouns that are used in a general sense (e.g., Dogs are loyal animals.)

For example, we would say "I need some water" (not "I need a water") and "I'm going to London" (not "I'm going to the London").
