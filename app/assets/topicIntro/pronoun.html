Pronouns

A pronoun is a word that stands in for a noun or noun phrase.

Pronouns are used to avoid repetition and to make sentences more concise and easier to read.

For example, instead of saying:

The dog is chasing the cat. The cat is running away from the dog.

We can use pronouns to say:

The dog is chasing it. It is running away from the dog.

In this example, the pronoun "it" stands for the noun "cat".

Types of pronouns

Pronoun type Usage Examples
Personal Refers to a person or thing without using a noun I, you, he, she, it, we, they
Possessive Shows ownership or belonging my, your, his, her, its, our, their
Demonstrative Points out something specific this, that, these, those
Interrogative Used to ask questions who, what, where, when, why, how
Relative Introduces a relative clause who, which, that, whose, whom
Indefinite Refers to a person or thing in a general way some, any, every, many, few, all, none, one, other, another
Reflexive Refers back to the subject of the sentence myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves
Reciprocal Refers to two or more people or things doing something to each other each other, one another

Examples of pronouns in use

Personal pronouns

  • I am going to the store.
  • Can you help me?
  • He is a doctor.
  • She is a teacher.
  • It is a beautiful day.
  • We are going to the park.
  • They are playing soccer.

Possessive pronouns

  • This is my book.
  • That is your car.
  • His dog is barking.
  • Her cat is sleeping.
  • Its tail is wagging.
  • Our house is big.
  • Their children are playing.

Demonstrative pronouns

  • This is a pen.
  • That is a pencil.
  • These are books.
  • Those are chairs.

Interrogative pronouns

  • Who is that?
  • What is this?
  • Where is the library?
  • When is the movie starting?
  • Why are you crying?
  • How do you make a cake?

Relative pronouns

  • The man who is wearing a hat is my father.
  • The book which I am reading is interesting.
  • The house that is for sale is beautiful.
  • The woman whose dog is barking is my neighbor.
  • The person whom I met yesterday is a famous actor.

Indefinite pronouns

  • Some people like chocolate.
  • Any questions?
  • Every day is a new day.
  • Many people are coming to the party.
  • Few people know the answer.
  • All of us are going on a trip.
  • None of them are here.
  • One of the students is missing.
  • The other team is winning.

Reflexive pronouns

  • I hurt myself.
  • You can do it yourself.
  • He is talking to himself.
  • She is looking at herself in the mirror.
  • It is cleaning itself.
  • We are enjoying ourselves.
  • They are blaming themselves.

Reciprocal pronouns

  • Mary and John gave each other gifts on their wedding day.
  • The children are playing with each other.
  • The team members are helping one another.
  • The neighbors are always looking out for one another.
  • The friends are always there for each other.
