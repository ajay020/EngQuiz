Past Simple

The past simple tense is used to talk about actions that happened and finished in the past. It's the most basic past tense in English, and it's used all the time.

When to Use It:

  • Finished Actions: Use it for things that happened and are done.

    I ate pizza yesterday.

  • Past Habits: For actions you did regularly in the past.

    She played piano every Sunday.

  • Past Facts: For historical truths.

    The Titanic sank in 1912.

Sentence Structure:

It's simple! Start with the subject, add the past form of the verb, and finish the sentence.

  1. Positive:

    Subject + past form of the verb + others.

    They visited the museum.

  2. Negative:

    Subject + did + not + base form of the verb + others.

    They did not visit the museum.

  3. Interrogative:

    Did + subject + base form of the verb + others + ?

    Did they visit the museum?

Examples:

  • I watched a movie.
  • He studied for the test.
  • We traveled to Paris.
