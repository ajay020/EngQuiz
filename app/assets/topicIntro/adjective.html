Adjectives

An adjective is a part of speech that modifies or describes a noun or pronoun.

Adjectives provide information about the size, shape, color, origin, material, quality, or any other attribute of a noun or pronoun.

Types of adjectives

  • Descriptive Adjectives:

    These adjectives describe the characteristics of a noun, like its size, color, shape, or appearance.

    Examples: blue car, huge tree, round table

  • Quantity Adjectives:

    They tell you how many or how much of something there is.

    Examples: three books, a lot of rain, several cookies

  • Demonstrative Adjectives:

    These point to specific nouns, indicating whether they're near or far.

    Examples: this book, those shoes, that dog

  • Possessive Adjectives:

    They show ownership or possession, telling you who something belongs to.

    Examples: my cat, her car, their house

  • Interrogative Adjectives:

    These adjectives begin questions and help you find out more about a noun.

    Examples: which book, what color, whose keys

  • Exclamatory Adjectives:

    When you're excited or surprised, these adjectives express strong emotions.

    Examples: amazing movie, fantastic news, incredible journey

Position of adjectives

Adjectives can be placed before or after the noun or pronoun they modify. For example, you could say "a big dog" or "a dog that is big."
