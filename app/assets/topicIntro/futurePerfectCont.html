The Future Perfect Continuous

The future perfect continuous tense is used to talk about actions that will have been in progress for a certain period of time before a specific time in the future.

It is formed with the helping verbs will have been and the present participle of the main verb.

Subject + will have been + present participle of the main verb with -ing.

Examples:

  • I will have been working on my homework for two hours by the time you arrive.
  • She will have been studying for her test all day tomorrow.
  • They will have been living in their new house for a year by next Christmas.

Negative:

Subject + will not have been + present participle of the main verb with -ing

Examples:

  • I will not have been working on my homework for two hours by the time you arrive.
  • She will not have been studying for her test all day tomorrow.
  • They will not have been living in their new house for a year by next Christmas.

Questions:

Will have + subject + been + present participle of the main verb with -ing?

Examples:

  • Will you have been working on your homework for two hours by the time I arrive?
  • Will she have been studying for her test all day tomorrow?
  • Will they have been living in their new house for a year by next Christmas?

More Examples:

  • I will have been playing video games for two hours by the time my mom comes home.
  • She will have been practicing her piano for an hour before her recital tomorrow.
  • They will have been building their sandcastle for an hour by the time the tide comes in.
  • Will she have been baking cookies all day tomorrow when we arrive?
  • Will they have been watching TV for an hour by the time their dad comes home?
